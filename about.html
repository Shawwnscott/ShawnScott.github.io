About Me

I’m Ashanni Scott, a Data Engineer with a background in Finance and Information Systems, building reliable, production-grade data pipelines across cloud environments. Originally from Jamaica and now based in Texas, I bring a strong mix of technical rigor, business context, and operational discipline to data engineering work.

My foundation in Finance shapes how I approach data: with an emphasis on accuracy, traceability, and downstream reporting impact. I’ve since built on that foundation through hands-on experience designing and operating ETL pipelines using Python, SQL, Apache Airflow, AWS (Glue, S3, MWAA, IAM, Parameter Store, Secrets Manager), Snowflake, and dbt.

Professionally, I’ve worked across data analysis, data engineering, and platform-support roles — modernizing legacy workflows, implementing config-driven pipelines, and enabling secure, multi-environment deployments. My work spans batch processing, API ingestion, data modeling, and orchestration patterns designed for reliability and scale.

I value clean design, clear documentation, and systems that fail loudly rather than silently. I’m especially interested in building data platforms that support finance, operations, and analytics teams with trustworthy, well-governed data.

Outside of work, I enjoy strength training, gaming, and anime. A fun fact about me: I’ve deadlifted 500 pounds — a reflection of the same consistency and discipline I bring to engineering problems.
